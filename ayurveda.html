Ayurveda

Ayurveda is an Indian traditional medical stream which dates back to over 5000 years. It is a complete and time tested science of life.The great sages with their effort and devotion brought forth this system of traditional medicine which could aid to almost all conditions hampering human health including many medical emergencies in ancient times. With a customised approach Ayurveda treatments and therapies for the same disease differ from place to place, person to person and so on.

When the contemporary medical science considers health as the mental, physical and social well-being, Ayurveda adds spiritual health also, to its topmost priorities. Ayurveda prescribes the body to be an epitome of universe and thus, works based on principles of biophysics.

In India, Ayurveda is now being taught in graduate level of education with 5.5 years duration and also postgraduate level specialisations are also provided on different branches of ayurveda for a duration of 3 years, such as kayachikitsa( general medicine), shalyatantra ( orthopaedics and general surgery), prasuti tantra evam striroga ( gynecology and obstetrics), swasthavritta (preventive and community medicine) etc.
